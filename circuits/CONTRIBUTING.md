# Contributing to `circuits/` — addendum

This directory carries stricter rules than the rest of the repository.
Everything here is `security-critical` by default.

## Hard requirements for every circuit change

1. **A written soundness argument.** A PR touching constraints must include
   a document (in `circuits/docs/`) stating what the circuit proves, the
   exact public-input layout, and why the constraint system admits no
   witness outside the intended relation. "Tests pass" is not an argument.
2. **Two approving reviews**, at least one from a designated circuit
   reviewer. No self-merge, no exceptions.
3. **No novel cryptography.** Groth16, Pedersen commitments, and
   established Merkle/nullifier patterns only. If your change needs a new
   primitive, open a design issue first.
4. **Verifying keys are artifacts, never sources.** Keys are generated by
   `scripts/build-artifacts.sh` from committed circuit sources plus the
   published ceremony transcript, and are reproducible by anyone.
5. **Layout lockstep.** The public-input layouts in `src/lib.rs::layout`
   are consumed by the contract layer (`shielded_pool`,
   `attestation_registry`). A layout change is a breaking protocol change:
   it requires new verifier instances and a coordinated release, and must
   say so in the PR description.

## Under-constraint is the threat model

The classic ZK bug is a constraint that is *missing*, not one that is
wrong — the circuit verifies honest proofs and also verifies dishonest
ones. Review accordingly: for each private witness, ask what stops a
malicious prover from choosing it freely.

## Trusted setup

The Groth16 setup per circuit is a public multi-party ceremony. Ceremony
tooling, contribution instructions, and the transcript live under
`circuits/ceremony/` (M1). Until the production ceremony has run, all keys
are development keys and must be labeled as such in `artifacts/`.
